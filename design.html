Design Documentation

Data Visual

I chose to use both line graphs and scatter plots to represent the Geomagnetic Storm (GTS) data from NASA for different reasons.
Line graphs are effective for showing trends and changes over time, making them suitable for illustrating how the intensity of GTS events varies across months and years.
In this context, they provide a clear visual representation of the temporal patterns in geomagnetic activity.
On the other hand, scatter plots are valuable for displaying individual data points, making them useful for pinpointing specific storm events and their corresponding intensities.
This combination of line graphs and scatter plots allows for a comprehensive exploration of GTS data, offering insights into both long-term trends and isolated events, thereby enhancing the understanding of geomagnetic storm occurrences and their impact.


Styling

The design of this project was inspired by NASA and outer space-related themes, with the choice of colors like blue and white reminiscent of the vast cosmos and the Earth's atmosphere.
These colors not only evoke a sense of exploration but also create a visually appealing and calming atmosphere for users.
A consistent font style was employed throughout the project to maintain a cohesive and professional appearance, ensuring that the information is easy to read and navigate.
In the design section, I implemented a scrollable function to create a gallery for images.
This feature was crucial to maintain a clean and organized layout, preventing images from appearing cluttered on the page.
Users can now effortlessly scroll through the gallery, providing a more user-friendly experience and ensuring that the visual content is easily accessible and visually engaging.
The design choices were made not only to pay homage to NASA's aesthetic but also to enhance user experience and readability.
